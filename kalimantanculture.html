The Culture's of Kalimantan

Ethnic Groups

Dayak: The indigenous Dayak people are known for their unique cultural practices, including traditional longhouses and elaborate tattoos. They are traditionally animistic, with a strong connection to nature and ancestor worship.
Malay: Predominantly found in the coastal areas, the Malay people have a rich cultural heritage that includes traditional music, dance, and cuisine. They are known for their involvement in trade and their influence on the region's history.
Iban: A subgroup of the Dayak, the Iban are renowned for their elaborate ceremonies and intricate beadwork. They are also known for their traditional longhouses and headhunting history.

Traditional Arts and Crafts

Weaving: Borneo is known for its traditional weaving techniques, producing textiles like the Dayak's intricate ikat patterns and the Malay's colorful songket fabric.
Wood Carving: Traditional wood carving is a significant art form in Borneo, with intricate designs used in ceremonial objects, masks, and architectural elements of longhouses.
Beadwork: The Iban and other Dayak groups are famous for their detailed beadwork, which is used in clothing, jewelry, and ceremonial attire.

Music and Dances

Gong Music: Traditional music often features gongs and other percussion instruments. The gongs are used in various ceremonies and celebrations, and their sound is considered an important part of cultural rituals.
Dance: Traditional dances in Borneo are performed during festivals and ceremonies. For example, the Dayak perform dances that tell stories of their ancestors, while the Malay have dances that are integral to their cultural celebrations.
