Kalimantan's Fascinating Facts and Figures
Facts about Kalimantan

Third-Largest Island

Borneo is the third-largest island in the world, after Greenland and New Guinea. It spans approximately 743,330 square kilometers (287,000 square miles).

Rich Biodiversity

Borneo is known for its incredible biodiversity. It is home to some of the world’s oldest rainforests, with diverse flora and fauna. Notable species include the endangered Bornean orangutan, the pygmy elephant, and the unique Rafflesia arnoldii, which produces the world’s largest flower.

Diverse Ethnic Groups

The island is inhabited by a variety of ethnic groups, each with distinct cultures and languages. These include the Dayak, Malay, Iban, Bajau, and others. The cultural diversity adds to the rich heritage of the island.

Major Rivers

Borneo is crisscrossed by several significant rivers, including the Kapuas River (the longest river in Indonesia), the Mahakam River, and the Barito River. These rivers are crucial for transportation, trade, and agriculture in the region.

Environmental Challenges

Borneo faces serious environmental challenges, including deforestation, habitat destruction, and the impact of illegal logging and palm oil plantations. These issues threaten the island’s unique ecosystems and biodiversity, prompting international conservation efforts to protect its natural heritage.
