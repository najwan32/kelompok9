The Geography of Kalimantan

Geographical Location

Kalimantan is located between 4° N - 4° S and 108° - 119° E. It is bordered by the Natuna Sea and Karimata Strait to the west, the Java Sea to the south, the Makassar Strait and Sulawesi Sea to the east, and Malaysia (Sarawak and Sabah) and Brunei to the north. Kalimantan is also divided into five provinces: West Kalimantan, Central Kalimantan, South Kalimantan, East Kalimantan, and North Kalimantan.

Topography

Mountains: The northern and central parts of Kalimantan have mountains and hills that form natural borders with Malaysia. The Müller Mountains and Schwaner Mountains are the main mountain ranges in Kalimantan, located in the central part of the island.
Lowlands: Most of Kalimantan consists of lowlands and swamp forests, especially in coastal areas and along major rivers.
Rivers: Kalimantan is known for its complex and extensive river system. The Kapuas River (1,143 km) in West Kalimantan is the longest river in Indonesia. The Mahakam River in East Kalimantan and the Barito River in South Kalimantan are also major rivers that serve as main transportation routes.

Climate

Kalimantan has a tropical climate with high rainfall throughout the year, although there are seasonal variations. The average temperature ranges from 24°C to 33°C. The region experiences two main seasons:
Rainy Season: Lasts from November to March, characterized by high rainfall.
Dry Season: Lasts from April to October, but some areas still experience sporadic rain.

Ecosystem

Kalimantan is home to vast tropical rainforests, peat swamps, mangroves, and lowland forests. These forests have high biodiversity, including rare and endemic species of flora and fauna. These ecosystems also act as important carbon sinks that help mitigate climate change.

Natural Resources

Kalimantan is rich in natural resources such as timber, coal, oil, natural gas, gold, and other minerals. East Kalimantan and South Kalimantan are known as centers of the coal mining and oil industries. In addition, Kalimantan has forests with valuable hardwood species, such as ironwood and meranti.
