Kalimantan's Exotic Plants and Animals
The Flora's and Fauna's of Kalimantan

Rafflesia arnoldii

Known for producing the world's largest single flower. It is a parasitic plant with a distinctive odor similar to rotting flesh, which attracts pollinators like carrion flies.

Meranti

Meranti is a type of tree that is important in the timber industry. Meranti wood is used for various purposes, such as furniture making and building materials. This species thrives in the forests of Kalimantan.

Black Orchid

The black orchid is one of the notable orchid species from Kalimantan. This flower is distinguished by its unique characteristic of having a black lip on its blooms, making it highly valued by ornamental plant collectors.

Orangutan (Pongo pygmaeus)

One of the great apes, critically endangered, and native to Borneo's rainforests. They are known for their intelligence and distinctive reddish-brown fur.

Bornean Pygmy Elephant (Elephas maximus borneensis)

A smaller subspecies of the Asian elephant, known for its unique features, such as smaller size and larger ears. They are found in the lowland forests of Borneo.

Proboscis Monkey (Nasalis larvatus)

Known for its large, distinctive nose, this monkey is native to Borneo’s mangrove forests and riverine habitats. It is also recognized for its social behavior and large stomach, which helps digest its food.
